Why phishing works

Phishing emails impersonate someone you trust to make you act fast — click a link, open an attachment, or hand over a password — before you stop to check. This short course shows you the tells, then checks that you can spot one.

  • A sense of urgency ("your account will be closed in 24 hours").
  • A sender address that doesn't match the real organisation.
  • Links whose visible text hides a different destination.
